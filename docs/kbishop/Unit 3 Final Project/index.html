What is Neurology?

Neurology is often described as the study of the nervous system-- made up of the central nervous system (CNS) and the peripheral nervous system (PNS)-- through a medical and scientific approach, delving into the functions, anatomy, and organic disorders among the system.

What is a Neurologist?

A neurologist caters to diseases manifested in the nervous system, which can span anywhere from movement, cerebrovascular disorders, seizure, spinal cord, headache, or speech and language disorders. In the treatment of such diseases, various treatments and tests can be done to diagnose patients; imaging tests such as MRI, CT, and PET scans are frequented in the field, as are lumbar punctures (a test conducted to analyze spinal fluid) and electroencephalograms, also called EEGs (the testing of electrical activity in one's brain using electrodes applied to the scalp).
