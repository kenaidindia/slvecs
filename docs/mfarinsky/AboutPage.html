About Culinary Arts

Culinary Arts originated in the Western World as a craft and later became a field of study. It began to evolve in the end of the Renaissance period. Chefs worked in castles before Monarchy was phased out as a modality which is when they took their craft to inns and hotels, and from there it evolved into a study. A lot of the study of Culinary Arts in Europe was organized by Jean Anthelme Brillat-Savarin. A similar path in Asia led to a seperate study of Culinary Arts which later merged with Western Counterpart. Modern Culinary Arts students study a wide variety of aspects of food. Specific areas are butchery, chemistry and thermodynamics, visual presentation, food safety, human nutrition and physiology, international history, the manufacture of food items. Training in culinary arts is possible in most countries usually at a University level.
